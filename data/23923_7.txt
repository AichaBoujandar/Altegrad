À Propos de L'ESME Sudria

   Fondée en 1905, l’école d'ingénieurs ESME Sudria forme en 5 ans des
   ingénieurs pluridisciplinaires, prêts à relever les défis
   technologiques du XXIe siècle : la transition énergétique, les
   véhicules autonomes, la robotique, les réseaux intelligents, les villes
   connectées, la cyber sécurité, et les biotechnologies.
   Trois composantes font la modernité de sa pédagogie : l’importance de
   l’esprit d’innovation ; l’omniprésence du projet et de l’initiative ;
   une très large ouverture internationale, humaine et culturelle. Depuis
   sa création, près de 15 000 ingénieurs ont été diplômés. L'école
   délivre un diplôme reconnu par l'Etat et accrédité par la CTI.

   Learn French Learn French while completing scientific studies

Spoken by 260 million people on 5 continents, French is the official language
of 70 nations as well as many international organizations. At ESME Sudria,
you will have a chance to learn French fast with tailor-made courses led by
professional instructors.

   Ouverture école ingénieur à Bordeaux Study abroad in France with the
   Erasmus Program

Designed by the European Commission, the Erasmus program aims at supporting
exchanges between higher education institutions in Europe and around the
world. ESME Sudria is a signatory to the Erasmus Charter and partners up with
several Erasmus partner universities.

5 years to become a certified engineer

   Founded in 1905, the ESME Sudria Engineering School is a private,
   non-profit higher education institution under the supervision of the
   French ministry of education and research. We have over a century of
   experience in training engineers. Our programs provide a broad,
   well-developed foundation which enables our graduates to succeed in a
   wide variety of engineering careers.

   At ESME Sudria, our mission is to train engineers, through
   state-of-the-art research, to face the technological challenges ahead:
   energy transition, new mobility, digital intelligence and health and
   human services. We also work with industrial and international
   partners. This collaboration advances scientific knowledge, and
   broadens our curriculum.
